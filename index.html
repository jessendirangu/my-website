Cancer Awareness & Health Education

What is Cancer?

Cancer is a disease where abnormal cells grow uncontrollably and can spread to other parts of the body.

Common Causes & Risk Factors

  • Smoking and tobacco use
  • Poor diet and lack of physical activity
  • Alcohol consumption
  • Genetic factors (family history)
  • Exposure to harmful chemicals or radiation

Common Types of Cancer

  • Breast cancer
  • Lung cancer
  • Prostate cancer
  • Cervical cancer
  • Colorectal cancer

Signs and Symptoms

  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Persistent fatigue
  • Lumps or swelling
  • Chronic pain
  • Changes in skin or unusual bleeding

Prevention

  • Maintain a healthy diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol
  • Regular medical checkups
  • Vaccination (e.g. HPV for cervical cancer)

Early Detection

Screening tests such as mammograms, Pap smears, and colon screening help detect cancer early when treatment is more effective.

Treatment Options

  • Surgery – removing the tumour
  • Chemotherapy – using drugs to kill cancer cells
  • Radiotherapy – using radiation
  • Immunotherapy – boosting the immune system
  • Targeted therapy – attacking specific cancer cells

What To Do

If you notice unusual symptoms, seek medical advice immediately. Early diagnosis saves lives.
